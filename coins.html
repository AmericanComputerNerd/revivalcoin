Spots (SPT)

Spots is a cryptocurrency built to facilitate the trade of virtual currency for precious metals. The currency's name is in reference to the Silver / Gold Spot, which is the terminology used to refer to the trade price of precious metals.

Spots had a broken client, which basically froze the block chain, and the former developer had moved on to other projects. The Revival Coin Group relaunched the coin, forked the client to fix the issues and switch the algorithm to Scrypt-N.

WeAreSatoshi (WAS)

WeAreSatoshi is a coin designed to pay homage to the creator of bitcoin, Satoshi Nakamoto. The coins main goal is to remind the cryptocurrency community that any one of us has the power to change the world for the better.

The Revival Coin Group reached out to the original Dev and received his blessing to take the coin over. The coin was forked to X11 from SHA256, and the money supply was reduced by 1 / 1000 through a coin swap.

Icebergcoin (ICB)

Icebergcoin was introduced by a group of four developers that chose to remain anonymous. They brought to the table a coin with a unique, low-energy algorithm and an escrowed coin distribution through Bittrex. After seeing the coin through the initial PoW phase and selling approximately $50,000 worth of coins, the developer disappeared.

Revival Coin Group attempted to reach the developers, but did not get through. We are in the process of moving all open source material to secure locations and working to ensure the coin remains stable despite the developer's hiatus.
